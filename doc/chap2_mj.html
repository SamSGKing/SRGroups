2.1 Regular rooted tree groups

2.1-1 IsRegularRootedTreeGroup
‣ IsRegularRootedTreeGroup( arg )( filter )

Returns: true or false

Groups acting on the regular rooted trees \(T_{k,n}\) are stored together with their degree \(k\in\mathbb{N}_{\ge 2}\) (see RegularRootedTreeGroupDegree (???)) and depth \(n\in\mathbb{N}\) (see RegularRootedTreeGroupDepth (???)) as well as other attributes and properties in this category.

2.1-2 RegularRootedTreeGroupDegree
‣ RegularRootedTreeGroupDegree( G )( attribute )

Returns: The degree of G.

The argument of this attribute is a regular rooted tree group G.

gap> RegularRootedTreeGroupDegree(AutT(2,3));
2

2.1-3 RegularRootedTreeGroupDepth
‣ RegularRootedTreeGroupDepth( G )( attribute )

Returns: The depth of G.

The argument of this attribute is a regular rooted tree group G.

gap> RegularRootedTreeGroupDepth(AutT(2,3));
3

2.1-4 RegularRootedTreeGroup
‣ RegularRootedTreeGroup( k, n, G )( operation )

Returns: The regular rooted tree group G as an object of the category IsRegularRootedTreeGroup (???), together with its degree k (see RegularRootedTreeGroupDegree (???)) and its depth n (see RegularRootedTreeGroupDepth (???)).

The arguments of this method are a permutation group G \(\le\mathrm{Aut}(T_{k,n})\), its degree k and its depth n.

2.2 Auxiliary functions

2.2-1 BelowAction
‣ BelowAction( k, n, aut, i )( function )

Returns: The restriction of aut to the subtree below the level 1 vertex i, as an element of \(\mathrm{Aut}(T_{k,n-1})\).

The arguments of this function are a degree k \(\in\mathbb{N}_{\ge 2}\), a depth n \(\in\mathbb{N}\), an element aut of \(\mathrm{Aut}(T_{k,n})\) (see AutT (3.2-1)), and a depth 1 vertex i \(\in\{1,\cdots,k\}\) of \(T_{k,n}\).

gap> BelowAction(2,2,(1,2)(3,4),2);
(1,2)

2.2-2 RemoveConjugates
‣ RemoveConjugates( G, subgroups )( function )

Returns: Removes G-conjugates from the list subgroups.

The arguments of this function are a group G and a list subgroups of subgroups of G.

gap> G:=SymmetricGroup(3);
Sym( [ 1 .. 3 ] )
gap> subgroups:=[Group((1,2)),Group((2,3)),Group((1,3))];
[ Group([ (1,2) ]), Group([ (2,3) ]), Group([ (1,3) ]) ]
gap> RemoveConjugates(G,subgroups);
gap> subgroups;
[ Group([ (1,2) ]) ]
