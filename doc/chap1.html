1 Introduction

Let G be a subgroup of \textrm{Aut}(T_{k,k}) with its group action, \alpha, defined as \alpha(g,x)=g(x), where g\in G are the automorphisms of G and x\in X the vertices of T_{k,k}. Let \textrm{stab}_{G}(0)=\{g\in G : \alpha(g,0) = 0\}, and T_0\subset T_{k,k} be the set of all vertices below and including the vertex 0. Additonally, let \varphi_0 : \textrm{stab}_G(0)\rightarrow G be a group homomorphism with the mapping g\mapsto g|_{T_0}. Then G is called self-replicating if and only if the following two conditions, \mathcal{R}_k, are satisfied: G is vertex transitive on level 1 of T_{k,k}, and \varphi_0\left(\textrm{stab}_{G}(0)\right)=G.
